# Findings Overview

The main empirical takeaway is that explicit smart-buy language is not especially common in government plans, even though a broader retrieval-and-LLM read finds more plans that appear to describe the same ideas. When we step back from the smart-buy list altogether, governments more often emphasize inclusion, access, higher education, and emergency response.

## High-Level Findings

- The strict search works best as a lower bound of direct, explicit mentions.
- The broader retrieval-and-LLM layer increases counts, but does not overturn the overall pattern.
- Pre-primary becomes more prominent when French-language plans are added.
- The broadest whole-corpus read suggests that many plans talk more about inclusion, access, emergency response, and higher education than about the smart buys directly.
